About me

Researcher.
Engineer. Enthusiast.

My name is Duc Duy Nguyen — you can call me Joey. I am a PhD candidate at the Norwegian University of Science and Technology (NTNU), where my research centres on ammonia and hydrogen as clean-burning fuels for marine engines.

My work sits at the intersection of experimental combustion science and maritime decarbonization. Using a single-cylinder optical engine equipped with a hydrogen pre-chamber jet ignition system, I investigate lean-burn ammonia-hydrogen mixtures, combining high-speed optical diagnostics — natural luminosity, OH* and NH* chemiluminescence — with pressure analysis and FTIR emissions measurement.

Prior to NTNU, I served as a graduate researcher at the Clean Combustion Research Center (CCRC) at KAUST in Saudi Arabia, studying hydrogen combustion behaviour in CFR engines, and at the Korea Institute of Machinery and Materials (KIMM), where I developed hydrogen-powered engines for Power-to-Gas systems.

My industrial career spans five years as a project and facilities engineer across Vietnam — at Terumo BCT, ON Semiconductor (now Onsemi), and HOYA Lens — giving me a grounded perspective on bringing research from laboratory to real-world application. I hold a Master of Engineering in Environment & Energy Mechanical Engineering from the University of Science and Technology (UST) in Korea, and a degree in Thermal Engineering from HCMUTE in Vietnam.
